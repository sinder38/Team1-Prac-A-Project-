Sprint Retrospective - Week 2

What Went Well

Product Owner completed sprint planning documents early.
Technical analysis was completed successfully with support, resistance, and trendline identification.
Data verification ensured consistency between calculations and predictions.
Human Score analysis was completed with a clear override rationale and confidence assessment.
Team members successfully created and submitted pull requests for their work.

What Did Not Go Well

Some team members were unclear about their responsibilities at the beginning of the sprint.
Team communication on Discord was limited.
Progress updates were not always provided promptly.
GitHub workflow knowledge varied across team members.

Blockers Encountered

Difficulty understanding role responsibilities, particularly for Human Score analysis.
Additional time was required to verify technical calculations and ensure consistency with prediction logic.
Limited team engagement slowed information collection and coordination.

Scrum Master Actions

Followed up with team members for progress updates.
Recorded blockers and tracked sprint progress throughout the sprint.
Monitored completion of required deliverables and team submissions.
Encouraged team members to communicate progress and raise issues when needed.

Improvements for Next Sprint

1. Clarify all role responsibilities at the beginning of the sprint.
2. Request progress updates earlier in the week.
3. Increase communication through Discord.
4. Encourage members to create Draft Pull Requests earlier.
5. Improve GitHub workflow understanding across the team.

Scrum Master Reflection

The team completed several important deliverables for Week 2, including sprint planning, technical analysis, data verification, and Human Score evaluation. While overall progress was positive, communication and coordination remain areas for improvement. More frequent updates, clearer role expectations, and earlier collaboration will help reduce last-minute work and improve sprint efficiency in future sprints.
